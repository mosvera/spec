## Becoming a maintainer

Mosvera welcomes new maintainers. The criteria are deliberate and
documented so the path is legible to any contributor.

To become a maintainer, you need:

1. **Sustained contribution over at least 3 months.** This can be
   code, MEPs, ADRs, documentation, examples, or review activity —
   any meaningful surface of the project. The intent is to
   demonstrate reliability and engaged judgment, not to hit a
   specific quota.
2. **A sponsor.** An existing maintainer publicly nominates you,
   speaking to your judgment and reliability.
3. **Approval by simple majority** of existing maintainers.

There is no employer requirement, no geographic requirement, and no
time-zone requirement. The criteria are about contribution and
judgment, not about identity.

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers have authority over the surfaces they maintain and
shared responsibility for the project's health overall. The core
responsibilities are:

- **Review pull requests** in your scope. Reviewers approve or
  request changes; they do not block trivially.
- **Shepherd MEPs and ADRs** through the process. This includes
  asking the research-grounded questions Doctrine 5 expects.
- **Hold the doctrines.** Public-first, adoption-over-convenience,
  spec-neutrality, comparable-class framing, research-first. If a
  PR or proposal violates one, name it.
- **Be reachable.** Maintainers do not need to be on call, but they
  should respond on a meaningful cadence to issues and PRs in
  their scope.

## Stepping down

Maintainers may step down at any time by editing this file and
opening a PR. Stepping down does not require sponsorship or
approval; it is a personal decision.

## Inactivity

Once the project has a Technical Committee (post-1.0), the
inactivity rules will be formalized in alignment with the OTel TC
attendance pattern — see
[`GOVERNANCE.md`](./GOVERNANCE.md#target-model-at-10). During
the single-maintainer phase, inactivity is handled informally: a maintainer who
becomes unreachable for an extended period may be moved to an
"emeritus" status by the remaining maintainers, with the option to
return on request.
